Made with ♥ in Baltimore

There are serious problems with Healthcare in the U.S., many of which would be rapidly improved if we had fluid, interoperable data and powerful tools to use it.

I believe the only way to fix some of the most fundamental flaws in the system is to focus on building solutions which directly engage patients. Patients are already the conduit through which the most critical information flows, and once our technology reflects that, we will start to change the world.

I'm also a major advocate of open-source technology. The federal government has already invested billions in building systems which run entire hospital operations, all of which are freely available. If the industry can easily collaborate with government, we can recycle that investment for the industry at large. That's what I'm working on.
